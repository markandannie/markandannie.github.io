The Ranch

We recently found the town of Roslyn, WA as the site for our first home.
And 10 miles to the east you can find Flying Horseshoe Ranch.

Annie attended summer horse camp here as a kid, and experienced her first taste of the open Ponderosa forest, rolling hills, and Kittitas Valley fields. The ranch has been family owned since the 1950s, shifting from a kids camp to a guest ranch about 15 years ago. Their property enjoys views of the Teanaway Valley and includes 100+ acres of forested trails up the hill—ample space to roam.

3190 Red Bridge Rd, Cle Elum WA 98922

Lodging

Flying Horseshoe Ranch sleeps about 100 people, and we would love to have every bed filled!
Accommodations range from bunk houses to canvas tents with beds & linens, all while maintaining a rustic environment with shared bathrooms and nature all around.

ON-RANCH OFFERINGS:

OFF-SITE OPTIONS:

If you must stay off-site, check out our suggestions below. Roslyn is a lovely town to explore, and it hosts a Sunday Farmer’s market—not to be missed. Ronald is just west nearer to Lake Cle Elum, and hosts many seasonal rentals. Suncadia also has many rental options, including multi-bedroom houses for larger groups.

Worth a look:
Hotel Roslyn: we have reserved four rooms for family. Get in touch if interested. Spruce Moose Inn: downtown Roslyn. Huckleberry House: historic Roslyn inn. Iron Horse Inn: sleep in a train caboose.

FAQ

Where are Mark and Annie staying?
Flying Horseshoe Ranch!

What should I pack?
Sunblock, water bottle, bathing suit, and your dancing shoes.
If you stay on the ranch, you may need to bring your own pillow and bedding (depending on the booking). See Lodging for more info.

When should I arrive?
We are partying all weekend, from Friday afternoon to Sunday. The ceremony is at 5pm on Saturday. Check out the Schedule for more info.

Can I bring my kids?
Yes! Plan accordingly if they will be sleeping at the ranch (travel crib or their own bunk bed, etc.). They would be welcome at every activity and would have a great time!

Can I bring my dog?
Sorry, but the ranch host has requested no dogs.
Check out Waggin Tails Ranch or Bord & Pillar Pet Resort for boarding.

What is the dress code?
No camp uniforms required. Annie says "summer casual festive" whatever that means.

Any dangerous flora or fauna?
Mountain Lion, Northern Pacific Rattlesnake, Yellow Sac Spider, Black Widow Spider, Black Bear.
Don't leave any food in the cabins.
Also avoid Poison Ivy and Poison Sumac.

Do you have a registry?
Yes, sort of. The greatest gift you can give us is your presence and your help. We’d love to keep the camp vibe going with lots of participation, and would appreciate any help you can offer! Check out the Registry section for more info.
